About the Congress

Sri Lanka's Premier
Earth Sciences Forum

RESCON 2024 is the premier annual research congress organized by the Postgraduate Institute of Science (PGIS), University of Peradeniya — Sri Lanka's foremost institution for postgraduate education in the natural sciences.

The congress brings together researchers, academics, industry professionals, and policymakers to share cutting-edge findings across Earth, Mineral, and Environmental Sciences, fostering interdisciplinary dialogue that addresses pressing global challenges.

RESCON serves as an essential platform for graduate students and early-career researchers to present their work, receive expert feedback, and build lasting professional networks within Sri Lanka and internationally.

Submission Information

Author Guidelines

Submission Platform

All abstracts must be submitted through the Microsoft CMT Conference Management System. Create a free account at cmt3.research.microsoft.com before submitting.

Abstract Format

  • Maximum 250 words (excluding title and author details)
  • Structured format: Background, Objectives, Methodology, Results, Conclusions
  • Font: Times New Roman, 12pt, single-spaced
  • No images, figures, or references in the abstract body
  • Clearly indicate the conference theme track (A–F)

File Submission

Upload as a .docx or .pdf file. Filename format: RESCON2026_LastName_TrackA.docx

Download Abstract Template

Oral Presentations

  • 10 minutes for the presentation
  • 5 minutes for Q&A and discussion
  • Slides must be in PowerPoint (.pptx) or PDF format
  • Submit slides to session chairs 24 hours before your session
  • Use 16:9 widescreen format, minimum 24pt font size

Poster Presentations

  • Poster size: A0 Portrait (841mm × 1189mm)
  • Authors must be present at their poster during designated sessions
  • Posters will be displayed throughout both conference days

General Notes

All presenters should arrive 15 minutes before their session. The conference venue provides LCD projectors, laser pointers, and laptop connectivity (HDMI).

All submissions are subject to double-blind peer review. Authors must ensure their work is original, unpublished, and not under review elsewhere. Plagiarism checks will be conducted on all abstracts.

Accepted abstracts will be published in the RESCON 2024 Proceedings (ISSN assigned). Selected papers may be invited for full publication in the Sri Lanka Journal of Environmental Sciences.

By submitting, authors grant PGIS non-exclusive rights to publish and distribute the work in print and digital formats.
