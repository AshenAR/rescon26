Transforming Crop Breeding with Technology and Adaptive Strategies for Resilient Varieties

📅 22nd September 2025 (Monday) 🏛️ PGIS Conference Hall 🌱 Crop Science · Climate Resilience

The 5th Pre-RESCON Workshop, themed Transforming Crop Breeding with Technology and Adaptive Strategies for Resilient Varieties, was successfully held on 22nd September 2025 at the Postgraduate Institute of Science (PGIS), University of Peradeniya. The event brought together a diverse group of researchers, breeders, technologists, policy-makers, and postgraduate students for a day of engaging discussions on how emerging technologies can reshape crop breeding pipelines.

The inauguration ceremony commenced with the lighting of the oil lamp, followed by opening remarks by Dr. H. A. C. K. Ariyarathna, Workshop Coordinator; Prof. M. Y. U. Ganehenege, Congress Chair of RESCON 2025; Prof. R. G. S. C. Rajapakse, Dean of the Faculty of Science; and Prof. B. M. N. W. B. Balasooriya, Director of PGIS.

The workshop featured a keynote address by Dr. Miceal Francki, who highlighted the pivotal role of modern genomic and data-driven tools in developing stress-tolerant crop varieties for future food security. Throughout the day, participants engaged in technical sessions and panel discussions exploring key themes:

  • Integration of technology in rice breeding workflows
  • Breeding for climate resilience and abiotic stress tolerance
  • Policy frameworks to support technology adoption in national breeding programs

The workshop concluded with a panel discussion and closing ceremony, where participants formulated key recommendations for enhancing national breeding programs through technological innovation and interdisciplinary collaboration.

Emerging Trends of Clean Energy & Opportunities of Higher Studies in Europe

📅 17th September 2025 (Wednesday) ⏰ 1:00 PM – 3:00 PM 🏛️ PGIS Auditorium ⚡ Energy · Hydrogen · Higher Studies

We were pleased to announce the 4th Pre-Conference Talk for RESCON 2025, delivered by Professor Upul Wijayantha (UK), a leading researcher in clean energy and hydrogen technologies. All students and early-career researchers were encouraged to attend.

Professor Wijayantha, Head of the Hydrogen Integration Research Centre at Cranfield University and Co-Director of the EPSRC EnerHy CDT at Loughborough University, shared insights from his three decades of research. The session addressed current trends in clean energy, the role of hydrogen in decarbonisation, and the exciting opportunities available for young scientists pursuing higher studies in Europe.

The session was successfully held with the participation of over 90 attendees, generating vibrant discussion on energy futures and career pathways in the European research ecosystem.

Navigating Graduate Studies & Effective SOP Writing

📅 2nd August 2025 (Saturday) ⏰ 9:00 AM onwards 🏛️ PGIS Conference Hall 🎓 Graduate Studies · Career Guidance

The 3rd Pre-Conference Workshop of PGIS RESCON 2025 was held on Saturday, 2nd August 2025, at the PGIS Conference Hall with over 100 participants, including undergraduates, postgraduates, and early-career researchers. The workshop offered practical guidance for students planning higher studies and careers abroad.

The program opened with a ceremonial procession and remarks by Prof. M.Y.U. Ganehenege, Chairperson of RESCON 2025, followed by an introduction to PGIS and RESCON by Prof. Nanda Balasooriya, Director of PGIS.

Academic proceedings began with Prof. Lunte's research talk on "Bioanalytical Applications on Microchip and Capillary Electrophoresis", highlighting applications of modern separation technologies in pharmaceutical research. The event concluded with a cultural performance by the PGIS Art Circle.

How to Navigate Through the Academic Challenges

📅 19th June 2025 (Thursday) ⏰ 1:00 PM 🏛️ PGIS Conference Hall 🧠 Mental Health · Resilience · Career

Students and early-career academics often face intense pressure that can hinder their academic and personal growth. This seminar explored practical strategies and motivational insights derived from the speaker's personal experiences, focusing on overcoming common obstacles encountered during academic journeys.

Key themes addressed during the seminar:

  • Academic pressure and its impact on performance and mental health
  • Effective time management and prioritization techniques to enhance productivity
  • Setting realistic goals and maintaining motivation under pressure
  • Practical methods for overcoming procrastination and cultivating self-discipline
  • Resilience frameworks for navigating failure and rejection in academia
  • Career pathways and future planning strategies for researchers

The overarching goal was to inspire and empower attendees to thrive amidst pressures, harnessing their potential for both academic success and personal fulfilment in challenging times.

Intermittent Fasting, Neuroplasticity, and Brain Health

📅 28th March 2025 (Friday) ⏰ 2:00 PM – 3:00 PM 🏛️ PGIS Auditorium 🧬 Neuroscience · Diet · Brain Health

The RESCON 2025 Pre-Conference Series was officially launched with an engaging and well-attended public lecture on "Intermittent Fasting, Neuroplasticity, and Brain Health", delivered by Professor Thiruma V. Arumugam, an internationally renowned neuroscientist and Professor of Physiology at La Trobe University, Australia.

This insightful session explored how intermittent fasting influences neuroplasticity and brain function, drawing on cutting-edge research to highlight how dietary patterns can impact cognitive performance, neurodegeneration, and healthy brain aging.

Professor Arumugam heads the Healthy Brain Ageing and Dementia Laboratory at La Trobe University, Melbourne. His research focuses on the molecular mechanisms behind neuronal death in conditions such as stroke and vascular dementia. The lecture provided participants with:

  • A scientific overview of intermittent fasting and its physiological impacts
  • The latest insights into neuroplasticity and aging-related cognitive decline
  • Evidence-based discussions on the relationship between diet and brain function
  • A vibrant Q&A session engaging practical and research-oriented aspects

This public lecture was an outstanding kickoff to PGIS RESCON 2025's pre-conference events, reaffirming the Institute's commitment to bridging cutting-edge science with public knowledge and health awareness.
